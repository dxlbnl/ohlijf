# The Trust-Feel-Release Framework

The core proprietary mechanism that OhLijf teaches is the "Trust-Feel-Release" method. The copy heavily anchors on this framework to simplify the complex scientific concepts of neuroplasticity and Mindbody Syndrome.

> 🔗 **Source File:** [src/routes/(site)/methode/+page.md](file:///home/dexter/Projects/Web/ohlijf/src/routes/(site)/methode/+page.md)

## 1. Trust (Vertrouwen opbouwen)
The content emphasizes an evidence-based approach. The founders explicitly mention their TU Delft background to build immediate authority and counter the skepticism often associated with psychosomatic treatments (*"We hebben geleerd altijd naar harde cijfers te kijken"*).
- **Core Action**: Psychoeducation and visualisations.
- **Relatability**: Acknowledging that ADHD brains tend to over-analyze and doubt, providing science-backed mental imagery to circumvent this.

## 2. Feel (Ontspan en voel emoties)
Moving from the parasympathetic *fight-or-flight* to *rest-and-digest*.
- **Core Action**: Processing repressed emotions instead of bottling them up.
- **Messaging**: Highlighting the constant "alert" state neurodivergent women naturally sit in, offering permission to physically down-regulate.

## 3. Release (Laat gaan wie je denkt te moeten zijn)
The transformation stage, referred to affectionately as "version 2.0".
- **Core Action**: Breaking behavioral patterns like people-pleasing and perfectionism.
- **Messaging**: "Het gevoel dat alles kan en alles mag" (The feeling that everything is possible and allowed). Removing the intense internal pressure that creates the psychosomatic feedback loop. 

## Copywriting Approach
The narrative intentionally blends academic reassurance with conversational warmth. It addresses the user's ADHD traits (e.g., "overvolle popcornhoofd") directly, framing those exact traits not as broken pieces, but as the very things causing their unique pain presentation.
